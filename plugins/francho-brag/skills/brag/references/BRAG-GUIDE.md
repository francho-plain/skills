# Brag Document — Instructions & Best Practices

> Based on [Julia Evans' guide to brag documents](https://jvns.ca/blog/brag-documents/)

## What is a brag document?

A brag document is a living record of your accomplishments, contributions, and growth over time.
Its purpose is to make your work **visible** so that it is recognised during performance reviews,
when asking for a promotion, or simply to remind yourself how much you have achieved.

## Why keep one?

- Performance reviews happen infrequently; memory is unreliable.
- Your manager may not be aware of everything you do — this document helps them advocate for you.
- It is easier to write an accurate self-evaluation when you have a running log.
- It helps you identify patterns and gaps in your career growth.

## How to use this document

### Update frequently

Add entries as things happen — don't wait until review season.
A quick note written the same week is far more accurate than trying to reconstruct it months later.

### Be specific and impact-focused

For every accomplishment, try to answer:
- **What** did you do?
- **Why** did it matter? (impact on the team, product, company, or users)
- **How** did you do it? (skills, tools, approaches used)

Avoid vague statements like *"helped with the project"*.
Prefer specific ones like *"led the migration of service X to Y, reducing latency by 30%"*.

### Include both big and small wins

Don't filter out things that feel small. Patterns of small wins matter.
Examples of things worth recording:
- Shipped a feature or fixed a tricky bug
- Wrote or improved documentation
- Onboarded or mentored a colleague
- Gave a talk or wrote a blog post
- Raised a problem that led to an important decision
- Received positive feedback from a user, peer, or manager

### Keep the document honest and data-driven

Where possible, include numbers, percentages, or other measurable outcomes.
Quote feedback verbatim (with permission) so you have concrete evidence.

### Share it

Share a (possibly edited) version with your manager before performance reviews.
This is not bragging — it is helping your manager write an accurate assessment of your work.

## Document structure

The recommended structure for your brag document is:

```
# Brag Document — Francho

## Goals for this year
## Goals for next year

## [Year] Accomplishments

### Projects
### Collaboration & Mentorship
### Design & Documentation
### Company / Community Building
### Learning
### Outside of Work
### Feedback Received
```

Each section is optional — only add the ones that are relevant to you.

## Review cadence

| Frequency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Weekly    | Add any accomplishments, feedback, or learnings from the week |
| Monthly   | Review entries; add impact/outcome for completed work |
| Quarterly | Share a summary with your manager |
| Yearly    | Archive the year's section; start a new one |

## Tips

- Use past tense and active voice: *"Designed …"*, *"Led …"*, *"Reduced …"*
- Link to pull requests, documents, or tickets as evidence where relevant.
- Include work that did not ship — research spikes, cancelled projects, and rejected proposals still demonstrate effort and skill.
- Record conference talks, blog posts, or open-source contributions even if they are outside your main job.
